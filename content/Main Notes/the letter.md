

I have wanted to tell you the following news for some time. I decided to put it into a letter so I am able to articulate my thoughts and to let you read back over them.

You will have noticed that I am significantly happier and healthier this past year. There is a reason for this that I have wanted to tell you for a while, but have always backed down at the last minute.

I am transgender. Specifically, I identify as non-binary and transfeminine.

This may be difficult to understand and may come as a suprise to you, but it is something I have known for a few years now. As of the start of this past year I have been openly presenting as such amongst my friends and peers. I currently go by the name El, and I use they/them or she/her pronouns. I understand this is a big change and may not come naturally, but it would mean so much to me if you would try your best. I understand there will be slip-ups and mistakes, and this is okay. You don't need to make a big deal out of it, a correction will be enough to show you care and respect who I am.

I do not identify with he/him pronouns or male-gendered language. I identify far closer with feminine language, and the label of 'girl' than I have ever done with that of 'boy'. This has been the case for as long as I can remember, it is only in the past few years that I have been able to understand and articulate it. 

I also want to tell you that I am undergoing hormone replacement therapy, or HRT, and I have been doing so for around half a year at this point. You will notice changes to my body as it becomes the one that feels correct for me. 

I would like to make it clear that this is a decision that I have thought on for many years. It is not one that I make lightly. It is not a 'choice', it is that I have made the decision to live openly as my authentic self. I would not choose to be transgender, but this is the truth of who I am, and I am no longer able to deny it. It is not because of trauma, or upbringing, or ideology or anything else you may have heard people imply. It is simply the fact of who I have always been.

This is a scary time to be trans. Though the world is a more accepting place in many ways, there is currently a lot of hatred levelled against people like myself. I believe it is important that I do not allow this to deter me from pursuing my transition and being my true self. I would ask, however, that you do not share this information with others, including our family, without my consent. I'm sure you understand that many will not understand and may not be accepting, and I would like to think about how I want to navigate this on my own terms. I am okay with you referring to me in the old manner when appropriate to ensure this. I am placing a lot of trust in you and I would appreciate it if this was honoured.

In many ways this does not change much. I am still the same person I have always been. I have the same interests, the same dreams and goals. It is common for parents to see this as the 'loss' of their son, but instead, I would like you to try and think of this as me feeling safe to be open as who I have actually been all this time, and not hiding this facet of myself.

I am sure you have a lot of questions and perhaps concerns, and I would like to answer them. I am also happy to provide you with resources to help too understand my identity better and to help you to support me going forward, and to navigate your own feelings on this. 

I cannot deny that even now I am scared for what may think or feel about this news. But, I know that you have always loved and supported me and right now I need that more than ever.

I love you so much,
El
