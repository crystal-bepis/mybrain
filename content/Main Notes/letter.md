It's still hard to explain exactly who I am - identity isn't the easiest thing to define at the best of times - but I think a decent way you'll probably be able to understand is that I see myself as somewhere in the realm of both Alice and Cosmo rolled into one. 

This is possibly not a surprise to you. You may have had your suspicions about it, what with Vamp (who was a bit of a way to test the waters as a nonbinary character) and more concretely, in recent months with Byways, heh. Roleplay helped a lot with this, even prior to our introduction, but there's only so far some shitty smut rp can get you, and it's not exactly a way to help feel like a real *person*. And from there, each response, new character, new conversation, they helped to form the person I actually am, the person confident enough in themselves to come out and be visibly me.

Anyway, the purpose of this message is to thank you for being here. If I'm honest, I've been absolutely shitting it about this. It's really hard to assess how people will react, when so many who seem alright have changed their tune. But I think I know you enough to know that isn't what you'll do. Even if it is, the point stands.
Thank you for being here, providing me an outlet to be me for the years I didn't have another. Both in our stories - as you know, I put a lot of myself into my characters - but also just in general. 

You are one of the first people to know me as the person I actually am, not the person that a couple of decades of expectations tried to make me become. Without this, I really don't know where I would be right now. I definitely wouldn't be happily out and proud amongst my irl friends and aquiantences (still working on the whole family side of it). But it's said now, and no matter how you react, I want the main point of this to be that I'm grateful in ways this word vomit won't be able to really articulate.
