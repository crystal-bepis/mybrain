[[Byways]]

Alice sat with the small part of herself that regretted asking him that question, but she snuffs it out in favour of trying her best to imagine the scenes as Cassidy recounted that time gone by. Violette sounded like a loyal friend - And definitely seemed like she'd agree with Cosmo's ethos of scrapping with anyone who might slight their friends. 

The revealed knowledge the Astra had been the genesis of *Apricot's* name did flicker the hint of jealousy back in the girl's mind, but in a way it was sweet. Ultimately, Cassidy wouldn't be Cassidy if not for his past, and all who made that past what it was. 

High school sweethearts. Alice couldn't say she related. High school had been, in short, a tough time. She tended not to think about it all too much. It had been on her mind more often recently, since she'd checked the route map. It was almost a bit of a joke that Evanston and Pittsburgh were both on the way. The past, and the future. One always helps to create the other, after all. 

"That's cute. I'm glad you folks are still in touch after all that time." Alice let out a breath of smoke as she spoke, tapping the ash from the tip and passing it back to Cassidy. 
"I don't really talk to my old high school friends anymore. I guess we weren't too close, I was kind of new to Evanston when we started, and no one really stuck around after I..." She leaned back in her chair a little, picking up her own stick and idly prodding at the dirt by her chair. "I guess that's not fair. There was a couple, but we didn't stay *that* in touch, and I moved to San Fran for college. And then, y'know, when the buzzing got way worse, even the once-every-year-or-so contact went radio silent. I guess I just dropped off the map for them."

--

Alice did notice Cassidy's slight fluctuation of mood, like conjuring up these thoughts might be pulling on latent emotional strings that he had preferred lay dormant for now. It was understandable, given the circumstances. She understood apprehension at the idea of reconnecting with old friends. It was part of what prompted her to bring up her old schoolfriends instead of probing further herself - Though, it wasn't quite a change of subject.

She was glad he was able to pull her diatribe back around in a way that alleviated any concerns of word-vomiting at him. She'd said a fair amount of it before, she knew, in bits and pieces here and there. Cassidy was one of the few she'd opened up to, and even he barely knew much of the detail. She'd likely have told him if he asked, but Cassidy was good about that. 

A strange duality - In ways, she was both of his examples. Someone who was at arm's length, keeping much behind a curtain over the couple of years they'd known one another, and yet also someone who had so readily and happily basked in the warmth of his friendship from the very first time she was invited to the *Sunbeam*.

Before Alice could prod that thought further, Cassidy swings the subject, and she decided not to tug on the threads, instead pulling that smoke into her lungs as the joint warmed fingers, the glow now up near her fingers. 
"I'll toast to that. And in honor of Nav, I'll add in a toast to the quiet, slow way around, and to... Exploring the history in the quiet." She decided against vocalising the other item on the itinerary for both of them: *Places with a past, and not quite knowing whether you'll be encountering souls or ghosts.*

"So, you're bunking with Coz tonight, hm? If they're a bit much, you can just tell them you're trying to sleep, and they'll leave you alone. They'll probably pretend to be sad about it, but don't fall for it. Otherwise they might keep you up all night." Alice chuckled softly, shaking her head. "When Cosmo has energy and when they want to sleep for ten hours straight seems to be completely arbitrary, but they *can* be reasoned with. We need our Captain in his best form, after all. And we want Cosmo not to nap through whatever excursions Nav has planned for us."

--

"Ah- N-nah, it's cool. I know Cosmo is looking forward to getting to know you a bit better." Alice smiled, knowing full well that Cosmo would be wiggling their eyebrows at that phrasing. "And the kid seems okay. I mean, y'know. But I can handle him. I wouldn't mind getting to know someone else here, anyway. Assuming he's awake, I guess."

She takes one last toke on the joint before handing the stub to Cassidy, giving him the honour of the last couple of harsh, hot hits before the thing was spent. 
"Um, I feel- I guess I should- I," Alice caught a little on her words. She hated it, she always did her best to be considered with what she said, and while taking her time to form an answer was one thing - aloof, chilled out, cool, wise, whatever you wanted to ascribe - stumbling over your words just made you look silly.
"Just to be clear, Cosmo and I, we aren't- I mean, we're not-"
*Be normal!*
"We're not like a *couple* or anything. I mean, not like-" Alice felt her cheeks reddening. This was probably the worst possible explanation she could have given, fuelled by a mix of not wanting to undersell who Cosmo was to her, but also not wanting to make Cassidy assume she was spoken for. It'd be a bit rude for her to have never told him about her having a partner this whole time - And Cosmo wasn't that. They were just... Cosmo. 
She was somewhat aware that her explanation was probably leaving more questions than it answered.
"-Not like in a relationship. But yeah. We're close. They helped me a lot when I moved here for college. And they understood the whole..." She gestured to herself - a loose jacket hid the figure, but Cassidy would know her slender frame well - "...Thing, and then when the buzzing got worse, it turned out they understood that as well. And they're good at being there for you. Comforting you, distracting you, making the buzz go away for a bit. I'm not sure where I'd be if not for them. No offence to you or anything, you've helped a lot in the same way..." 
She ran her fingers through her hair awkwardly, gazing into the smouldering fire. 
Maybe not *quite* in the same way.

--

Alice hadn't quite fully registered what Cassidy was saying when he extended his hand to her. "Huh- O-oh..." She trailed off as she took his hand almost on instinct, and was pulled in close.

Her heart felt like it paused, just for a moment. She hadn't expected this. It was of course far from the first time she'd hugged Cassidy, but something about this felt a bit different to most. The last time she felt an embrace like this from him would have... Probably been when she had broken down when she told him about the childhood dog who'd just passed, or at least her brother had finally been able to tell her. Or more similar to this moment, when she had finally felt she was safe enough to talk directly about herself - Where he'd known all words weren't quite right, when just still being there had been enough.

Alice's fingers tenderly clutched at the fabric of the back of Cassidy's shirt, her head tucked in to the crook of his neck. She felt tension leave her shoulders that she had never noticed was there. The slow rise and fall of his chest against her, the warmth of his arms around her like a protective blanket, a reminder that he would be here if she needed him. She didn't need his protection, really. She was a big girl who'd looked after herself long enough.

But she did need him. It wasn't just Cosmo - In many ways, she wasn't sure where she'd be without *him* too. And obviously she shouldn't do anything to upset the balance, to possibly push him away for the sake of a childish crush.
The best thing for it was to just enjoy this long, quiet moment.

Once they pulled away, Alice held his gaze, her own not-quite smile greeting his own. It would be hard to place her emotions, but gratitude was chief amongst them. Gratitude and a deep sense of tranquility, like for this moment, the buzzing had slipped away.
"Sure thing. I think the bathroom's probably free by now. And I think I'll be more comfortable than if I were sharing a bed with Sienna." She hadn't seen the commotion, but it hadn't exactly been a quiet argument.

Alice looked for a moment longer, then broke the gaze with a bashful grin. She took a step towards the *Sunbeam*, past Cassidy, before the briefest of hesitations. The crush got the better of her, just a little. A tiny gesture that could be as ambiguous as she wanted.
Cassidy would feel a very quick, very gentle peck on the cheek, closer to his ear than his mouth, followed by her quiet voice, softer than usual. The same words she had said to him just before they boarded for their trip.
-## "Thanks for this."

-

Cosmo was upside down on the couch, head hanging over the floor. They gave Alice a big grin as she came in, flopping sideways to right themself, their hair even messier than usual thanks to what may well have been an extended inversion. "You're happy." They remarked, eyes creasing to show this fact had made them happy in turn.
"Yeah." Alice rubbed her arm coyly, remembering the warmth of his arms. "That obvious?"
"The lights dance a'lil when you get happy." Cosmo nods, closing their eyes contently. "Just enough that I can see 'em."

Alice smiled to her feet. She never knew that. 
"Sweet dreams, Starburst. Don't keep him up *too* late." She passes Cosmo by, giving them a well-received tousle of the hair, and a little kiss on the forehead - A move that Cosmo much enjoyed, butting their head up a little in the same way they did with their hair - And then moved through to the central cabin, straight to the bathroom to get as cozy as she could.

Nav undid her spacebuns, fingers flexing to slip the hairbands onto her wrist, giving Sienna a smirk in the dresser mirror.
"Tell me something I don't know. But I told myself I ain't gonna be the one to make the move." She chuckled, turning to face the other girl. Her silvery hair, when down like this fell just short of her collar, framing her face nicely. It'd be the first time Sienna would see her like so, as buns or a ponytail were her standard affair when working, or out and about.

"I'm starting to regret it, though. I worry you might beat me to the punch if I don't step up." She smirked, taking a seat on the side of the bed that seemed to have been designated - likely rather begrudgingly by Sienna - as hers. Nav had chosen a silky pair of sleep shorts, and a comfortable sports bra-like top, opting to continue her tradition of keeping that toned midriff exposed - A trade-off, in her mind, as Nav would personally choose to forgo a top entirely, in the comfort of her own room. 

She placed her phone down on the headboard storage area, though she had already turned it off hours prior. She considered turning it on, checking in with the family, looking at her group chats, anything like that, but she wouldn't. She'd already told them all at great length that they could expect to barely hear from her - As if she was voyaging to Antarctica, not down some dirt tracks through her national backyard. She takes a beat to massage in the dots of white moisturiser she had applied to her face in the mirror just prior, eyes wandering over the bizarre cannabis-like plants that adorned the wall. She hadn't spent all too long in Cassidy's room before - *Sigh* - but these had always fascinated her, their idiosyncratic nature unlike any of the plants she had seen throughout the years, be them in boy's bedrooms or nestled amongst tomato plants in unassuming greenhouses. Not that she saw them all too often, but these were clearly not quite... Normal.

"Though, I gotta say, we might have a sleeper pick on our hands." Nav remarked conspiratorially. "Looks like Alice might be hunting that title herself. I bet Cassidy wouldn't say no." Nav still wasn't sure where she stood with Sienna. She didn't catch the same ire as Ike, but if their shifts at the *Río* taught her anything, it's that could come on fast as a squall if one were to say something perceived to slight her. Gossip, however, seemed to be a language the girl understood.

"Not that I blame him. She's cute, in a weird, apocalyptically melancholic way. Can't imagine what it's like, though. Thought *my* ears ringing after standing next to one too many amps was bad enough."

--

Nav cringed as Sienna mentioned Ike. She sighed, flopping onto the bed as well, head turned to look to the other girl.
"God, okay, fair enough. I think that one might have been a miss." She muttered, fiddling with her hair.
"We met at some party, or afters, or some shit like that. I think he has buddies in the gig scene, but I barely saw him out at shows." Nav couldn't truly remember exactly where it was herself. It wasn't a slight against the boy, but it was indicative of Sienna's point - She barely really knew the guy. As such, she wasn't exactly about to defend his character, but... She didn't particularly want to fuel this particular fire under the trip.
"I remember we got on pretty well. Got chatting, ended up talking most of the night. At some point, one of us mentioned, uhm, 'magic', and that's when we realised we were the same." She shrugged a little, gazing at the odd cannabis plants on the wall, and then back to Sienna.
"I mentioned it on a whim when I first thought up the trip. He left me on read, I highkey assumed he just thought the whole thing was wack. I was shocked he showed up."
