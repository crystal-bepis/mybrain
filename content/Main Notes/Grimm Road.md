The snow-white creature that was curled around the branch of the tallest tree in the woods twitched an ear at the telltale sound of the guild doors being thrown open. While there wasn't a way to sense the personal subtleties of the action in the same way she could at a closer distance, as with the gait of a walk - there was no mistaking who would have been responsible for *that*. Ann felt a little guilty. She had known he would react this way, it was part of the fun. But, she truly did just... Need some air. A chance to run through nature, albeit a facsimile, but one that felt close enough to home to work.

*Home...*

Enough of that. No hypothesising over the next place to take that mantle, or wistful recollection of the first. There was an important task at hand, and she couldn't rightly up and leave until it was put to rest. No matter how she cut it, she wasn't able to completely extract herself from responsibility. She'd been with them, she had been an accomplice, and if she didn't put it right it would haunt her for the rest of her long life.

And... 
That poor man. Why did he have to be... Like the missing gear to a watch, that once slot into place, made everything tick as it should? 

It only made it worse. 

Hauser would find Anežka sat on one of the stumps, cross-legged. She gave him a quizzical expression, cocking her head a little, the vaguest hint of a smile on her lips, any trace of her prior pondering wiped from her face. She wouldn't give him the satisfaction of knowing that she both understood why he was panicked, nor that it was, in fairness, a justified fear.
*"You are looking like the Mad Hatter. And your shirt is buttoned wrong."*

--

Anežka managed to hide her smile as Hauser stammered his way through his excuse. *Panák. You fool no one*. She rose from the stump, sidling over to him.
*"Take it easy. It has been some time since I have been 'taking it easy'. Doing such a thing, at this time?"* She remarked once she was in front of him, looking up at him from under her hat, a slight grin on her lips.
*"If you eat alone, then who am I to eat with? It is not fair on myself, to have to eat alone because you-"*, she gave a gentle poke to his ribs, *"Are feeling sorry for yourself."*
A flash of white, and she brushed past him, but he would feel a tug on his shirt, coaxing him in her direction.
*"Fix your buttons. You are making it look as if you did not, ah, 'take it easy last night'.* She smirks. Part of her knew that she shouldn't be so blasé over the events of their evening together. But, it was better than addressing it head-on. This way, she could avoid thinking too deeply on it herself, could keep it casual. *Like it was.*
Instead, the game always continued.
